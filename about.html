01 — Story



I grew up taking things apart to see how they worked — not always successfully, but that never stopped me. Curiosity has always been my default setting. My first real ambition was to be an astronaut — I've always had a fascination with outer space — and when becoming an astronaut stopped being realistic, I just found other ways to put my mind to good use and feed my curiosity: I see something interesting and I follow it until I understand it.

I'm 23, self-taught in most of what I know, and I've spent the majority of that time thinking alone. No mentors, no community — just problems, stubborn determination, the internet and an unusual role model. I've always gravitated toward intelligent characters who build impossible things with complete conviction —Tony Stark— Not just for the spectacle, but for the mindset — the elaborate thinking and the refusal to be limited by what's available. Growing up without many resources reinforced that. Limitations stopped feeling like dead ends and started feeling like constraints to design around.

"I don't see science fiction as fantasy.
I believe I can make it reality. One project at a time."
